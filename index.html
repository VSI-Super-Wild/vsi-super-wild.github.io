Abstract

Humans can efficiently parse continuous sensory streams, from hours to years, scaffolding an internal world model that grounds spatial reasoning and prediction. To mimic this capacity, spatial supersensing challenges multimodal models to move beyond linguistic understanding toward true world modeling. However, their benchmark relies on synthetic long videos formed by concatenating random short clips and is mostly limited to household scenes, leaving real-world continuity and diversity underexplored. To address the gap, we introduce VSI-Super-Wild, a large-scale spatial supersensing benchmark built from genuinely long, in-the-wild videos across diverse scenarios. Inspired by cognitive studies on how humans structure experience, we systematically probe the full triad of world state: the agent (observer), objects (scene items), and the environment (places and global layout). In total, VSI-Super-Wild comprises 442 real-world long-form videos across 8 scene categories and 6,980 human-verified question-answer pairs. Evaluating multimodal models on VSI-Super-Wild exposes a fundamental disconnect: despite advances in static image understanding, models consistently fail at tasks that require coherent world-state tracking over time. We characterize how performance degrades with world-state complexity and temporal horizon, and diagnose four failure modes: spatial collapse, semantic shortcuts, insufficient update, and instance confusion.

Motivation: VSI-Super-Wild moves spatial supersensing from synthetic clip concatenations toward genuinely long, in-the-wild video streams that require coherent tracking of agents, objects, and environments.

Task Suite

VMR: Motion Orientation Recall

Infers camera motion orientation relative to viewing direction at a queried moment, probing agent-centric latent state recall.

VPO: Place Temporal Ordering

Orders yaw-rotated place frames from long panoramic videos, testing heading-invariant place memory across the environment.

VOO: Object Temporal Ordering

Orders queried objects by first or last occurrence, requiring self-updating object-state tracking through continuous streams.

VOC: Continuous Object Counting

Predicts unique-instance counts from long video, testing whether models can maintain a numerical object world state.

Overview of VSI-Super-Wild tasks and the expanded world-state anchors.

Data Construction

The pipeline starts from in-the-wild panoramic YouTube videos across culture and entertainment, industry, medical, office and education, residential space, retail space, street view, and transportation hubs. Panoramas are projected into four orthogonal perspective views, then enriched with temporal metadata from object proposals, YOLO-World filtering, and SAM3 instance masks, plus spatial metadata from Depth-Anything-3 camera poses.

Video CollectionEgocentric, dynamic, handheld recordings are filtered from broad real-world scenes.
Metadata GenerationTemporal object tracks and camera poses support task synthesis.
QA GenerationRule-based Q&A is verified by humans, with rollback when metadata needs refinement.

Key Findings
Overall PerformanceCurrent MLLMs still struggle with spatial supersensing in the wild. Gemini-3.1-Pro leads with 44.36 overall, while GPT-5.4 and the strongest open-source model, Cambrian-S-7B, reach 34.52 and 34.22.
Challenge: World State ComplexityObject-state probing is often more tractable than agent- and environment-state modeling. Open-source models score much higher on VOO than on VMR or VPO, exposing a gap in 3D spatial cognition.
Challenge: Longer Temporal HorizonsAverage performance drops from 35.0 in the 0-10 minute bin to 26.3 in the 120+ minute bin, showing that maintaining coherent world states becomes harder as observation time grows.
Failure Modes
Spatial CollapseModels fall back to view-specific 2D matching under viewpoint change.
Semantic ShortcutFrame-level semantics can replace true spatiotemporal motion reasoning.
Insufficient UpdateLast-occurrence ordering exposes weak state updates as later evidence arrives.
Instance ConfusionMotion blur, partial occlusion, and viewpoint change make object identity tracking unstable in the wild.

Shuffle24

Under Shuffle24, each multiple-choice question is evaluated across all 24 option permutations. VSI-Super-Wild shows lower Top-K and Pass@K, higher option-order bias, and a larger best-vs-worst permutation gap than VSI-SUPER, confirming that the in-the-wild long-video setting is more difficult and more sensitive to answer ordering artifacts.

Conclusion

VSI-Super-Wild evaluates whether MLLMs can conduct human-like implicit world modeling under real-world in-the-wild streams. Compared with previous supersensing benchmarks, it advances evaluation along two axes: real-world diversity and broader world-state probing. The updated benchmark contains 442 unconstrained online videos across 8 scene categories, with videos lasting over 4 hours at maximum, and 6,980 human-verified Q&As spanning object-, agent-, and environment-centric tasks.

Across 13 mainstream MLLMs, current models still fall substantially short of robust spatial supersensing in the wild. Models are relatively more reliable on object-centric state queries, but struggle with agent and environment components that demand stronger 3D spatial cognition. Continuous counting remains especially difficult because it requires precise numeric answers under continual state updates. Longer temporal horizons further degrade performance, and the recurring failure modes show that current MLLMs still over-rely on frame-level semantics instead of maintaining stable, queryable spatiotemporal world representations.
